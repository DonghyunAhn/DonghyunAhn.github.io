Research

My work sits at the intersection of geospatial AI and computational social science.

Research Themes

🛰️

Socio-economic Estimation from Satellite Imagery

Inferring wealth, poverty, and urban development directly from overhead imagery using computer vision and distributional adjustment — in regions where ground-truth data is scarce. Published in Nature Communications, CIKM, KDD, and AAAI.

🤖

Multimodal AI & LLMs for Geospatial Tasks

Using large language models and multimodal architectures as flexible reasoners over geospatial data, enabling cross-country generalization and autonomous knowledge generation from open satellite archives (GeoSEE, arXiv 2024).

🌡️

Climate Risk & Environmental Sensing

Applying machine learning to satellite-derived signals for environmental monitoring: urban heat estimation, fine-grained air temperature mapping, and climate risk assessment to support evidence-based policy in climate-vulnerable regions.

🌍

AI for Social Good

Designing AI systems that serve humanitarian goals — monitoring SDG progress, supporting disaster response, and surfacing insights for under-resourced regions through interdisciplinary collaboration with social scientists and economists.
